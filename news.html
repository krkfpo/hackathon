Britain's women's Olympic preparations in disarray: Why a gold medal may be out of reach

From Phil Neville's departure to a cancelled farewell match, Britain's preparations for The Tokyo Olympics have been poor. When it comes to the question of which team can challenge for the gold medal that the U.S. women's soccer team hopes to win in Tokyo, Great Britain is certainly one of them. After all, the English women's team included 13 players from the same England team that lost 2-1 to the U.S. women's team in the 2019 Women's World Cup, which had reached the semifinals in three consecutive tournaments. On top of that, the British women's team will also have some of the best women's sports in Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland, so it's no surprise that the British women's team is strong.

The British women's football team got off to a flying start

Great Britain got off to a flying start in the Women's Olympic football tournament by beating Chile 2-0 on Wednesday. It is worth noting that this is the second time that British football has participated in the Olympics as a united team following the London 2012 Olympics. Fifteen of the women's squad of 18 are from England, two from Scotland and one from Wales. In the 2012 London Olympics, Wales joined England in a united team with Ryan Giggs, Craig Bellamy, Daniel Sturridge and Aaron Ramsey, but the team failed to reach the quarter-finals. England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland competed in separate teams, but each failed to qualify for Tokyo. FIFA is understood to have said that joint participation would not affect the status of the four associations. England also wanted to form a united league, but this was rejected by the Scottish, Welsh and Northern Irish fa.
