About Our Sport

A detailed look at the history of the Club

Gillingham Football Club, known then as New Brompton FC, were formed at the Napier Arms pub in May 1893, but changed to the current name in time for the 1912/13 season.

The share capital was set at £1,500 and a plot of land, subsequently to become known as Priestfield Stadium, was purchased for £600. The club adopted professional status the following year and became founder members of the Southern League, winning the Division Two title in 1895 and gaining promotion by beating Swindon 5/1 in a test match (a prelude to today's play-offs).

They continued to play in the Southern League without any great success, in fact at the end of the season’s either side of the first World War, the team finished bottom of the table.

Club Honours:

  • League Two Champions 2012/13
  • Division Four champions 1963/64, runners-up 1973/74
  • Division Three runners-up 1995/96
  • Division Two play-off winners 1999/2000, beaten finalists 1986/87 (then Division Three) and 1998/99
  • League Two play-off winners 2008/09
  • Southern League champions 1946/47 and 1948/49, runners-up 1947/48

Record attendances:

  • FA Cup - 23,002 v QPR ( January 1948)
  • League Cup - 20,566 v Arsenal (September 1966)
  • Football League - 20,128 v Millwall (September 1950)
  • Highest average attendance: 12,576 (1951/52)
  • Biggest Football League win: 10-0 v Chesterfield (September 1987)
  • Biggest FA Cup win: 10-1 v Gorleston (November 1957)
